SKARN

SHARED KNOWLEDGE OF ADVANCED RESEARCH NODES
The SKARN project is an open-source research initiative focused on making enterprise-grade GPU computing accessible through reverse engineering, custom adapter design, and comprehensive documentation. We develop hardware solutions that enable researchers to deploy NVLink-capable GPU clusters at a fraction of traditional costs.

All designs, schematics, and documentation are released under the CERN Open Hardware Licence (CERN-OHL-P), ensuring free access to cutting-edge compute infrastructure for the research community.

Current Project Initiatives

DGX/HGX SXM4 & SXM5 Reverse Engineering
Developing adjustable PCIe x1-x16 interfaces for individual A100/H100 GPUs with programmable trace length compensation for motherboard compatibility.
HPE Apollo XL270d Platform Adaptation
Complete electrical characterization and standalone operation of 8× V100 SXM2 compute trays outside original chassis.
Compact High-Density Systems
Portable dual V100 configurations in 12×10×9" cases, quad V100 workstations, and 8× V100 rack builds with full documentation.
Custom PCB Adapter Development
JPCie to PCIe adapters, SFF-8654 breakout boards, and power delivery solutions for SXM2/4/5 platforms.
